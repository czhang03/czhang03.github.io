Sun of Arles

Hai Zi
To the south, to the south. Your blood flows neither lover nor spring, nor moon, not even enough bread, and even fewer friends. There were only suffering infants, devouring all.

My skinny brother van Gogh, oh van Gogh! Like great burst from the earth's core, like a volcano seeking no return, are these silk clothing or wheat fields, or the times you seek not to live, erupting out of you?

One of your eyes is enough to lighten this land, Yet you insist on opening a third -- the sun of Arles. Burning the night sky into a crude stream; burning the ground into a vortex; when you lift your yellow shaky hand, sunflowers blooms.

You invited all those who seek a spark in the flame, paint not the Mount Olivet: paint the harvest, paint the unquenchable flame. You cleanse lives, in the name of an aging god.

My red-headed brother, after this glass of absinthe, you may light this fire. Burn.
